# Day 16 Reflection/Thought – 100 Days of LeetCode

After consistently solving problems for 16 days, I've realized that competitive programming is less about memorizing solutions and more about recognizing patterns and building problem-solving intuition.

Some of the biggest lessons so far:

* Every difficult problem becomes manageable once it is broken down into smaller steps.
* Revisiting mistakes is often more valuable than solving new problems.
* Time complexity analysis has become a natural part of my thought process.
* Consistency matters more than solving a large number of problems in a single day.
* Many seemingly different problems are built on the same underlying patterns such as prefix sums, sliding windows, binary search, and dynamic programming.

The journey is still in its early stages, but these 16 days have already improved my analytical thinking, debugging skills, and confidence in approaching unfamiliar problems.

Looking forward to the next 84 days of learning and growth.
